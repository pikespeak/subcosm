<the_fix_design>
## Steering → score mapping (the core design)

`day.steering` shape is `{ branch, symmetry, hue }` (DayVector.ts / SteeringSchema). Goal `targetParam` is one of `conflict` / `density` / `symmetry` (the three authored genomes). The mapping (direction-aware, so a nudge can move the outcome BOTH toward and away from the goal):

| Goal targetParam | Genome | Steering lever | Why |
|------------------|--------|----------------|-----|
| `symmetry` (above 5) | crystalline | `day.steering.symmetry` | the symmetry steering dimension directly corresponds to the symmetry goal — a symmetry nudge is the player's "make it more faceted" lever |
| `density` (above 0.7 norm) | chaotic | `day.steering.branch` | branch is the structural-spread lever; a positive branch nudge widens/populates the frontier → raises measured density; negative → lowers it |
| `conflict` (below 0.4) | calm | `day.steering.branch` | branch loosens/tightens structure; a positive branch nudge raises measured conflict (away from the goal), negative calms it (toward the goal) |

`hue` steering is NEVER a scored lever (no genome goal targets hue; hue is the visual hint only). For an unknown targetParam the steering contribution is 0 (safe fallback unchanged).

## Already-folded steering — do NOT re-apply steerGain

The tick's `foldSteering` (src/server/core/tick.ts) already collapses the per-day steer aggregate to `MEAN × steerGain` ONCE (OQ3 / D-08) BEFORE calling `score(dayVector, genome)`. The folded values land on `day.steering`. So `score()` reads `day.steering.{branch|symmetry}` DIRECTLY — it must NOT multiply by `steerGain` again (that would double-count and break the single-fold guarantee). The plausible magnitude of the folded steering on a scored lever is roughly: `symmetry` ∈ about [-0.5, 0.5] (steerGain.symmetry 0.5 × mean amount [-1,1]); `branch` ∈ about [-1, 1] (no `branch` key in steerGain → fold gain 1 × mean amount [-1,1]). The contribution function normalizes whatever magnitude arrives through `tanh`-style saturation OR a hard clamp so an extreme value can never exceed the cap (see bound below).

## The bound (I-5: biases, never dictates) — the hard prohibition

The steering contribution is an ADDITIVE, CLAMPED offset to `measured` (computed BEFORE the achieved/degree comparison), capped at a small fraction of the goal's degree span:

`STEER_BIAS_CAP` [ASSUMED] = **0.15** — the maximum absolute shift steering may apply to `measured`, expressed as a fraction of the per-goal degree span (`degreeBounds(targetParam, threshold)` → `max - min`). For conflict/density the span is 1.0 (the 0..1 axis) → max shift ±0.15. For symmetry the span is `2 × SYMMETRY_DEGREE_SPAN` = 4 arms → max shift ±0.6 arms.

Why 0.15 is a true BIAS, not a DICTATE (justification, encoded as the prohibition test):
- A **borderline** day sits within 0.15 of the threshold (e.g. conflict 0.45 vs goal <0.40; density 0.66 vs goal >0.70 normalized). Full toward-goal steering can pull it across → the GAME-03 positive truth.
- A **clearly-failed** day sits MORE than 0.15 of the band past the threshold (e.g. conflict 0.90 vs <0.40 — 0.50 away; density 0.20 vs >0.70 — 0.50 away). Even MAXIMUM toward-goal steering (±0.15) can NOT reach the threshold → stays failed.
- A **clearly-achieved** day sits MORE than 0.15 the other way → MAXIMUM away-from-goal steering can NOT flip it → stays achieved.
- For symmetry (integer arms), a ±0.6-arm shift can flip a 5-arm borderline (threshold-equal, `> 5` currently misses) to clear `> 5`, but can NOT turn a 4-arm clear-miss into `> 5` (needs +1.0+), nor a 6-arm clear-achieve into a miss (needs −1.0).

The contribution is applied so it pushes `measured` in the goal's favorable direction for a positive toward-goal nudge and unfavorable for a negative one — i.e. it is direction-aware against `goal.direction`, and the clamp is symmetric `[-cap, +cap]`. `measured` is re-clamped to its own valid axis after the offset (conflict/density to [0,1]; symmetry stays a real number on the arm axis — degree already tolerates non-integers).

## What does NOT change

- `score()` signature, `measure()` signature, `OutcomeSchema`, `DayVector`/`Steering` contracts — UNCHANGED (day already carries steering; Outcome stays z.infer). Confirm no schema change is needed.
- `src/server/core/tick.ts` `foldSteering` — UNCHANGED (steerGain applied once there).
- The activity-driven base of each metric (`day.conflict` / `starCount` / `deriveArms`) — UNCHANGED; the steering contribution is ADDED to it.
- HUD legibility: the HUD (`src/client/cosmos/hud.ts`) already calls `score(frontier, genome)` and shows `measured` vs threshold + on-track glyph. Because the live acting-user re-synth folds the nudge into the frontier `day.steering` (render.ts), and the HUD now scores THAT folded frontier, the HUD readout will automatically reflect the steering-shifted `measured`/on-track state with NO hud.ts change. Do NOT modify hud.ts/game.ts — the legibility requirement is met by score() reading steering (the HUD is downstream of score). (If, during execution, the HUD frontier does not carry folded steering at the moment of scoring, STOP and report — do not silently add UI scope.)
