<objective>
Make the frontier ignite animation data-driven from the day's metrics (VIS-ANIM): replace the uniform `pulse = 0.55 + 0.45*sin(time*0.0022*speed)` (`src/client/cosmos/CosmosScene.ts:157`) — which reads as a harsh on/off blink ([0.10, 1.0]) — with a gentle shimmer baseline plus bounded `conflict→amplitude/hardness` and `energy→tempo` modulation that mathematically cannot strobe (D-03/D-04). Bake a static per-day metric signature into each frozen shell so a high-conflict frozen day looks different from a calm one without any per-frame motion (D-05).

Purpose: Per D-03 (per-metric mapping: conflict→hardness/amplitude, energy→tempo), D-04 (gentle shimmer, never strobe), and D-05 (frozen shells gain character, not motion), different days and communities animate visibly differently — judged side-by-side vs `docs/subcosm-universe-mock.html` for a high-conflict day, a calm day, and an AMA/energy-spike day. This is the paint-side half: no engine contract change, no snapshot re-baseline. The 60fps rule (only the frontier re-renders per frame) and prefers-reduced-motion (static frame) are preserved.

Output: A new pure `src/client/cosmos/ignite.ts` (`igniteParams`) with a unit-tested no-strobe bound; `CosmosScene.update()` rewired to call it reading `shells[0].meta.conflict` + a cached frontier-energy scalar; and `paint.ts` `drawShell` injecting a baked per-day signature before `bakeShell`.
</objective>
